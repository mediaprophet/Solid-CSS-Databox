# ADR-0002 — Databox topology, tenancy and storage-controller/ownership

- **Status:** Adopted
- **Date:** 2026-07-14
- **Decision owner:** DBX-02 (Hard lead)
- **Residual human review required:** legal-policy — the "organisation is storage controller, consumer is protected assignee/rights-holder" split and the non-waivable-consumer-rights encoding are legal-policy statements that must be sighted by the legal-policy workstream (R-12 boundary); privacy — the opaque-identifier and tenancy-isolation rules should be sighted by a privacy reviewer against isolation-and-privacy.md.
- **Sources adjudicated:** S-02 (Databox topology / avoid claiming personal-Pod ownership); S-24 (who is the LWS storage controller); architecture.md (Recommended topology, Resource layout, Control plane and data plane, Program profile); isolation-and-privacy.md (Trust boundaries, Tenant controls, Identifier and metadata leakage); README.md invariants 1, 2, 3, 5, 10, 12; standards-roadmap.md "Compatibility risks requiring isolation" (storage-controller vs consumer-rights bullet).
- **Consumed by / blocks prompts:** DBX-04 (topology/discovery surface), DBX-10 (opaque box identifiers / provisioning), DBX-11 (per-program tenant isolation). Also consumed by DBX-06, DBX-07.
- **Relates to:** ADR-0016 (integration plane and protected relationship/customer→box mapping — the server-side mapping this ADR mandates), ADR-0021 (encryption boundary — provider/program payload confidentiality below the ACL layer), ADR-0024 (Track separation), ADR-0025 (Solid interoperability guarantee — the advertised topology must stay standard-discoverable).

## Context

Before any authorization, credential or exchange decision can be made, the register must fix *what a Databox is* as a Solid entity and *who controls it*. S-02 asks whether the Databox is presented as a Solid storage, a resource subtree, or another advertised service, and warns against "claiming personal Pod ownership where the organisation is custodian". S-24 asks who the LWS storage controller is for an organisation-hosted Databox and answers: the accountable organisation normally controls the storage, the consumer is the protected assignee/rights-holder, and governance/redress/non-waivable rights are encoded **separately** from storage control. Getting this wrong in either direction is dangerous: calling the Databox the consumer's Pod would falsely imply Pod-owner control over institutional evidence and a right to overwrite append-only records (implementation-decisions.md warns Solid compatibility "does not imply … Pod-owner control over institutional evidence, or permission to overwrite append-only records"); but letting "the organisation controls storage" imply custody erases the consumer's legal rights is exactly the failure standards-roadmap.md flags — "Documentation and UI must not imply that institutional custody eliminates consumer legal rights or redress."

CSS 7.1.9 reality (DBX-01 §5): pod identifiers are **slug/name-derived, not random** — `SubdomainIdentifierGenerator`/`SuffixIdentifierGenerator` just `sanitizeUrlPart(name).toLowerCase()` (`src/util/StringUtil.ts:19-21`); the `IdentifierGenerator` interface takes a `name`. There is **no tenant/multi-realm concept** (grep `tenant`/`multi-realm` → none); the OIDC provider, signing JWK and account storage are server-wide, only storage-backend path isolation pre-exists. So opaque random box identifiers and per-program tenancy are net-new — but the random primitives already exist in-tree (`randomUUID()`, `randomBytes(64)`), so it is a *replace-the-generator, reuse-the-primitive* job (DBX-01 §5). The architecture doc already fixes the resource layout (`/boxes/{opaque-box-id}/…`), the ≥128-bit CSPRNG identifier rule, the never-reassigned rule, the protected server-side box→relationship mapping, and the control-plane/data-plane split. This ADR makes those normative and binds them to S-02/S-24.

## Decision

**1. A Databox is an advertised Solid storage subtree per program relationship — NOT the consumer's personal Pod (S-02).** Each Databox is a discoverable Solid storage/resource subtree (the `/boxes/{opaque-box-id}/` tree of architecture.md Resource layout) operated by the organisation for one declared program and one represented person/relationship (invariant 1). It MUST be discoverable through the standard Solid storage-description / `Link` surface (ADR-0025; DBX-01 §4 confirms `StorageDescriptionAdvertiser`/`Handler` is the extension point). Documentation, UI and RDF MUST NOT present it as the consumer's general-purpose Pod or wallet, and MUST NOT imply Pod-owner control over institutional evidence or a right to overwrite append-only records.

**2. Ownership split (S-24, MUST):** the **accountable organisation is the LWS storage controller / custodian**; the **consumer is the protected assignee / rights-holder**. These are distinct roles. The implementation MUST encode governance, redress and **non-waivable consumer rights separately from storage control**, so that "the organisation controls the storage" never resolves, in code, config, RDF or UI, to "the consumer has no rights, access, correction or redress over these records". Custody does not erase consumer rights (standards-roadmap.md). Consumer access, correction and complaint routes (architecture.md Consumer record awareness; R-14) are first-class and are not gated on storage-controller consent.

**3. Tenancy (MUST/SHOULD, from architecture.md + isolation-and-privacy.md):**
   - A **distinct origin per program is preferred** (`https://databox.woolworths.example/`); **program subdomains are acceptable** for a contracted platform (`https://woolworths.databox-provider.example/`); **path-only tenancy is discouraged**.
   - Whichever is chosen, **token audiences, cookies, CORS policies, browser origins and client registrations MUST be program-bound** (a token/cookie/registration for program A MUST NOT be honoured for program B). Path-only tenancy is discouraged precisely because it cannot bind those to the tenant; if a deployment nonetheless uses paths, the tenant MUST be resolved and validated *before* Solid authorization (isolation-and-privacy.md Tenant controls) and the program binding enforced by the composed authorizer (ADR-0025 narrow-not-broaden rule), never by path string alone.
   - Cross-tenant negative tests are mandatory (isolation-and-privacy.md; owned by DBX-11).

**4. Identifiers (MUST):**
   - The **box identifier MUST be generated from ≥128 bits of CSPRNG output**. It MUST NOT be, or be derived from, a name, email, loyalty number, student number, customer reference, or an **unkeyed hash** of any such value (architecture.md; invariant 2).
   - **Resource identifiers within a box MUST be independently random** so that receipt/submission volume cannot be inferred by incrementing a sequence (architecture.md).
   - **Identifiers are never reassigned** (architecture.md).
   - The provider keeps a **protected server-side box → program-local-relationship mapping**; the raw customer identifier never enters a public URI, connection credential, consumer vault, notification, log, metric or dashboard (invariant 2; isolation-and-privacy.md Identifier and metadata leakage). Opaque identifiers reduce accidental disclosure but are **not secrets** — knowing a URL never grants access (invariant 3; enforced by ADR-0025 authorization + 404-not-403).
   - CSS gap (DBX-01 §5): CSS pod identifiers are slug-derived today, so opaque random box IDs require a **new `IdentifierGenerator` implementation**; it MUST reuse the in-tree CSPRNG primitive (`randomUUID()` / `randomBytes`) rather than the `sanitizeUrlPart(name)` path. This is an explicit input to DBX-10.

**5. Control plane / data plane separation (MUST, architecture.md):** provisioning, customer matching, staff administration and key management form a **control plane**; Solid resource operations form the **data plane**. They MUST NOT share an unrestricted public API, and the control plane **MUST NOT act through an ordinary consumer token**. The control plane creates/suspends Databoxes, binds program-local identities, installs policies, rotates keys, revokes relationships and manages lawful deletion; the data plane accepts validated deposits/submissions, authorizes retrieval, emits receipts/notifications, prevents destructive changes and records evidence. Hosting-provider administration is a security threat controlled below the RDF ACL layer (invariant 10).

## Alternatives considered

- **Present the Databox as the consumer's personal Pod / wallet.** Rejected (S-02, implementation-decisions.md): it falsely implies Pod-owner control over institutional evidence and a right to overwrite append-only records, and it inverts the S-24 controller role. The Databox is a relationship-scoped organisation-operated subtree, not the person's wallet (README Purpose).
- **Treat "organisation is storage controller" as full ownership that subsumes consumer rights.** Rejected (S-24, standards-roadmap.md): this is the exact failure the roadmap warns against — custody must not eliminate consumer legal rights or redress. Rights are encoded separately from storage control.
- **Path-only tenancy on a single origin (`/tenants/woolworths/…`).** Discouraged (architecture.md, isolation-and-privacy.md): a single origin cannot program-bind token audiences, cookies, CORS or client registrations, so cross-tenant isolation depends entirely on application logic with no browser/transport backstop. Distinct origins or subdomains are preferred; path-only is a fallback requiring pre-authorization tenant resolution.
- **Derive the box identifier from a keyed hash of the customer reference (pairwise-deterministic).** Rejected: even a keyed hash risks correlation if the key leaks or is reused across programs, and it couples identifier issuance to customer PII. A fresh ≥128-bit CSPRNG value with a protected server-side mapping is simpler, non-correlatable and matches invariant 2. (An *unkeyed* hash is forbidden outright.)
- **Reuse the CSS slug-derived `IdentifierGenerator` for box IDs.** Rejected (DBX-01 §5): slugs are name-derived and would leak PII into URLs (invariant 2 violation). A new generator over the CSPRNG primitive is required.
- **Let the control plane act through a privileged consumer token for admin convenience.** Rejected (architecture.md, invariant 10): it collapses the plane separation, makes admin actions indistinguishable from consumer actions in the data plane, and puts provider administration above rather than below the ACL layer.

## Consequences

- **Positive:** Fixes the entity model and ownership so every downstream ADR (auth context, authorizer, provisioning, exchange) has an unambiguous subject and controller. Program-bound origins/audiences give browser and token isolation a transport-level backstop, not just application logic. Opaque non-reassigned identifiers with a protected mapping close the URL/log correlation surface. The controller/rights-holder split lets legal-policy work encode redress without renegotiating the storage model.
- **Negative / cost:** Opaque box identifiers require a net-new `IdentifierGenerator` (DBX-10) and break the CSS convention that a pod URL is human-readable/slug-derived — operators lose "guess the URL from the name" convenience (which is the point). Per-program tenancy (DBX-11) is substantially new work because CSS's OIDC/JWK/account storage is server-wide (DBX-01 §5); higher-assurance deployments may need separate instances per program (isolation-and-privacy.md). Preferring distinct origins raises deployment/DNS/TLS overhead versus a single origin.
- **Privacy & threat notes:** Directly closes isolation-and-privacy.md threat cases: *guessing box/resource identifiers* (CSPRNG ≥128-bit, independently random resources), *leaking identifiers through logs/metrics/error messages* (no PII in URIs/logs/mapping-exposed surfaces), *querying another program* and *changing the host/path of a valid token to address another program* (program-bound audiences + pre-authorization tenant resolution). Opens no new surface beyond the operational cost of running per-program isolation; the residual risk is a misconfigured path-only deployment, mitigated by the mandatory pre-authorization tenant-resolution rule.

## Failure behavior

Fail closed:
- If the tenant/program cannot be unambiguously resolved and validated from the request's origin/audience (or, for a discouraged path-only deployment, from the path *before* authorization), the request is denied — never defaulted to a tenant.
- A token, cookie or client registration presented against a program it is not bound to is rejected as if unauthenticated; the response follows the existence-hiding rule (404-not-403, DBX-01 §3; ADR-0025) so it cannot confirm another program's box exists.
- If the opaque-identifier generator cannot obtain sufficient CSPRNG entropy, provisioning fails rather than falling back to a slug/name-derived or lower-entropy identifier.
- A control-plane operation attempted through a consumer/data-plane token is refused; control-plane APIs are unreachable from the public data-plane surface.
- Any attempt to write to or overwrite an append-only institutional record via a "Pod owner" assumption is denied by the append-only decorator (ADR to be recorded; DBX-01 §4 `ReadOnlyStore` pattern) regardless of storage-controller identity.

## Open sub-questions / residual gates

- The **concrete per-program tenant isolation controls** (separate origins/audiences, client registrations, service identities, storage namespaces, signing keys, queues, logs, cross-tenant negative tests) are specified in principle here and in isolation-and-privacy.md but their build is owned by **DBX-11**; the protected box→relationship mapping registry is detailed in ADR-0016.
- The **new `IdentifierGenerator`** implementation (opaque box IDs over the CSPRNG primitive) and its wiring into `PodSettings`/provisioning is owned by **DBX-10**; this ADR fixes the identifier *rules*, not the class.
- The **precise encoding of non-waivable consumer rights / governance / redress separately from storage control** (data shapes, policy vocabulary, UI copy) is owned jointly by the legal-policy workstream (R-12/R-14) and DBX-06/DBX-07; this ADR fixes the *separation requirement* and the S-24 role assignment, and defers the legal mapping (no statutory interpretation here, per R-12).
- The exact discovery/description surface a generic client sees is fixed by **ADR-0025** (Solid interoperability guarantee); this ADR only requires that the topology remain standard-discoverable.
